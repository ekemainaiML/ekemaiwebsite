MATLAB/Simulink

MATLAB is a high-level programming language and environment for numerical computation, data analysis, and algorithm development. Simulink, an add-on to MATLAB, is a graphical tool for modeling, simulating, and analyzing dynamic systems. It supports block diagrams and integrates seamlessly with MATLAB, making it ideal for designing control systems, signal processing, and AI-driven simulations in engineering and scientific applications.

CAD Models

CAD models are 3D models that represent real-world objects and their relationships. They are used to design and simulate complex systems, such as buildings, vehicles, and industrial equipment. CAD models are often used in engineering, product design, and manufacturing to create accurate and realistic representations of products and systems.

Simulink Models

Simulink is a software tool for modeling, simulating, and analyzing dynamic systems. It provides a graphical user interface (GUI) for creating and manipulating models, enabling users to design, simulate, and analyze complex systems. Simulink is commonly used in engineering, product design, and manufacturing to simulate and analyze complex systems.

Model-Based Designs

Model-based design (MBD) is a methodology that uses models to guide the design process. MBD involves breaking down complex problems into smaller, more manageable parts, and then designing and testing each part independently. It helps to identify and address issues before they become a problem.
